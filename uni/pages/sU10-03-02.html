Central Saint Martins 2018

Degree show website

As part of a 3 person team, I volunteered to create the defree show website of Central Saint Martins' graduating class on the BA for gaphic and communication design.

Main objectives

High quality, large images •

Unbiased ordering of work •

Ease of access: contact details, portfolio •

Enjoyable explorability •

Some of the troubles of the previous sites: Not all students submitted images of their work, the ordering of it was biased, everyone's work had to fit under a small number of predefined themes, exploring the page to discover work was difficult as was finding links to portfolios and contact details.

Need-to-haves

Looking at the previous sites and their basic structure we found that each site needed to have the following items:

A search function which takes names and tags as user inputs

An unbiased description / assignment of tags for each students' work

An "about" section which contains the course leader's review of this year's graduating class

A press section

A visitor section with a map and general info

These are the following UX goals we set ourselves:

Give visitor a more visual impression of the student work and not overwhelm them them with the amount of students (show visuals instead of lists, when site loads)

Make act of exploring site enjoyable, fun and ideally addictive?

First prototypes

We used Google Tensorflow, an open source neural net to assign tags to images. The closer the score is to 1, the more confident the AI is of it's choice.
