unit 10 part III

 

 

objective ?

To create an archive of the work of this year's graduating BA Graphic & Communication Design course at Central Saint Martins.

 

outcome ?

I attempted to create a website that highlights student work through 2 different functionalities: An entertaining / unique way of browsing random images of student work and a directed search. See the reasoning behind this decision here.

Sketches on right-hand side

 

Highlight random organisation of content at start. Focus on images of work instead of list of names (like previous sites, see above). Focus on ease of searchability which should lead to a pleasant but also unexpected browsing experience.

 

Trying out different ways of creating a search that does not just show a blank page with a search bar in the middle. Use free real estate and idle minutes before user begins search to showcase student work. Random selection of work due to fairness issues (everyone should get equal amounts of exposure).

 

Once the user begins the search, what should they see?

 

What is meaningful to them?

 

List of student names and tags, since those are the main elements being used to create the search functionality? (HOW else could search functionality be generated? WHO creates tagging system and HOW are they assigned to student work?)

 

Where does the user see them?

 

Should they be able to interact with them?

Focus on visitor's role as "researcher" / connector. Each visit to the website adds value to the collection as each person can make subjective connections between the different works. It might also serve as a tool to plan / remember their visit to the degree show. See reference Werkbank, used as inspiration for this idea.
